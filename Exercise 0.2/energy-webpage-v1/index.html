Home Energy Facts

The average Australian household uses 15-20 kWh per day. Appliances account for about 30% of this consumption.

Energy Saving Tips

  • Set aircon to 24°C (summer) and 19°C (winter)
  • Use cold water for washing clothes
  • Switch to LED lighting
  • Unplug appliances when not in use
  • Turn off standby power - saves up to $100/year

Television Energy Facts

Modern LED TVs are significantly more energy-efficient than older plasma and CRT models. A typical 55-inch LED TV uses around 100-150 watts, costing approximately $50-80 per year to run in Australia.

💡

Energy Star Ratings

Look for the Energy Rating Label when purchasing appliances in Australia. More stars mean better energy efficiency. A 5-star rated appliance can save you hundreds of dollars over its lifetime.

Did You Know?

Standby power can account for up to 10% of your household electricity usage. Turning appliances off at the wall when not in use can save the average Australian household over $100 per year.

💡 Energy Saving Tips for Australian Homes

  • Set your air conditioner to 24°C in summer and 19°C in winter for optimal efficiency
  • Use cold water for washing clothes - it can save up to 80% of washing machine energy
  • Switch to LED lighting - they use 75% less energy than incandescent bulbs
  • Unplug chargers and appliances when not in use to eliminate standby power
  • Compare energy providers regularly to ensure you're getting the best rate
  • Consider solar panels - over 3 million Australian homes now have rooftop solar
